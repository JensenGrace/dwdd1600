Most Famous Signers

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress at the time of signing. Known for his bold and prominent signature, his name has become synonymous with the word "signature" in American culture.
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the 3rd President of the United States and was a leading figure in American Enlightenment and republicanism.
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned diplomat, inventor, and statesman. Franklin was the oldest signer at 70 and a central figure in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Adams
Advocate for independence and key member of the Continental Congress. Later became the 2nd President of the United States and was a staunch defender of the Declaration’s principles.
Samuel Adams
Influential leader of the Sons of Liberty and a driving force behind the push for independence. Known as the “Father of the American Revolution.”
Richard Henry Lee
Proposed the resolution for independence in the Continental Congress. His motion led to the drafting of the Declaration of Independence.
